# SOP For Cybersecurity Program

Our primary focus will be on the official and authentic websites of the respective educational institutions, where we will gather information on the non-degree certification programs only, which they offer in the areas of cybersecurity. If we are getting any information we will give the information, if we do not have a clear information we will put "Not Found".

---

## 1. Program Name

**Description:** We will capture the name of the certificate (Non-degree) program in this field. We will put the reference link on the next column.

**Keywords:** "Cyber Defense Education Certificate", "Cybersecurity Analyst Certificate"

---

## 2. Admission Contact

**Description:** This section captures the designated point of contact responsible for admissions into the institution’s nondegree Cyber Certificate programs. If nothing found then for the specific school admission details can be taken. The verified reference link for the contact information is also provided in the next column.

**Research with keywords:** "Admission contact", "Contact us", "Admission email", "Contact for admission", "Office of admission"

---

## 3. Program Type

**Description:** This section outlines the specific certificate course or program offered by the institution under the Cyber domain. A validated reference link supporting the program details is included. It should be Graduate or Undergraduate Certificate.

- Associate degrees are considered degree programs and should not be included. Only non-degree certificates are in scope.
- Certificates taken as part of a Bachelor’s program (e.g., Cyber Defense Education Certificate at Colorado School of Mines) can be treated as Undergraduate Certificate Programs, but confirmation is required on whether they are standalone or mandatory parts of a degree.
- Community College Default assumption would be Community colleges generally offer Undergraduate level programs only. However, each case must be double checked to ensure no graduate level offerings exist.

**Research with keywords:** "Graduate Certificate", "Cybersecurity Certificate", "Program Type", "Academic Program", "Certificate Program in Cybersecurity"

---

## 4. Program Focus

**Description:** This section summarizes the key subject areas, competencies, and thematic coverage emphasized within the Cyber Certificate program. A reference link is provided to substantiate the curriculum details. Can be found in the same page in Program Type. It can mainly be fetched from the course details of that particular program but in a short or only the keywords. No assumption should be made, things should be crystal clear.

**Research with keywords:** "Cybersecurity Architecture", "Data Security & Privacy", "Leadership & Strategy", "Defensive Cyber Operations", "Offensive Cyber Operations", "Cybersecurity Law", "Security Auditing", "Critical Infrastructure", "Certificate Electives", "Program Description"

---

## 5. Program Focus in Details

**Description:** This section is for the details of those keywords of the courses taken in the previous field of Program Focus. The reference link should be provided in the next column.

---

## 6. Program Level

**Description:** This section identifies the academic level of the nondegree certificate program, such as Undergraduate Certificate or Graduate Certificate. The associated reference link is included for verification. The below points should be kept in mind:

- Certificates taken as part of a Bachelor’s program (e.g., Cyber Defense Education Certificate at Colorado School of Mines) can be treated as Undergraduate Certificate Programs, but confirmation is required on whether they are standalone or mandatory parts of a degree.
- Community colleges generally offer Undergraduate level programs only. However, each case must be double checked to ensure no graduate level offerings exist.

**Research with keywords:** "Graduate Certificate", "Graduate Catalog", "Certificate Programs – Graduate", "Online Graduate"

---

## 7. Credential Awarded

**Description:** This section specifies the exact credential granted upon successful completion of the program—for example, Cybersecurity Certificate or Graduate Certificate in Cybersecurity. It must be Non Degree.

**Research with keywords:** "Graduate Certificate", "Cybersecurity, Graduate Certificate" (appears as the program title), "Certificate Programs – Graduate", "Graduate Catalog"

---

## 8. Prerequisites

**Description:** This section lists the courses or academic requirements that must be completed prior to admission to the certificate program. It can be years of experience or high school diploma. The reference link should be provided in the next column.

**Research with keywords:** "Program Prerequisite Course", "Admission requirements", "Required prior coursework", "Prerequisite"

---

## 9. Courses

**Description:** This section details the number of required courses and credit hours included in the certificate program. Just put the Number of courses. Put the names in the single cell and separate by comma. The reference link should be provided in the next column.

**Research with keywords:** "Graduate Certificate in Cybersecurity", "Required Courses", "Certificate Electives", "Choose Three Courses"

---

## 10. Class Duration

**Description:** This section specifies the duration of each class session for the cybersecurity certificate program. The details can be in "Credits" or in "Units" only. The reference link should be provided in the next column. (example: 10 Credits, 29.5 Units)

**Research with keywords:** "Course lengths", "Course duration", "Credit Hours" (program structure context)

---

## 11. Minimum Completion Time

**Description:** This section outlines the minimum amount of time required to complete the certificate program. Time must in days, weeks or months. Minimum completion time should reflect real world duration, accounting for academic calendars and breaks. We will only use realistic minimum completion duration.

**Research with keywords:** "Course lengths", "Course duration"

---

## 12. Program Flexibility

**Description:** This section defines the attendance mode options (e.g., hybrid, inperson) available to students. If it is both, capture that as well. It mainly means if the student has options as per their requirements. How flexible the program is for the students? "Synchronized (The time or venue is fixed) or asynchronized (The time or venue is not fixed)".

**Research with keywords:** "Synchronized", "Asynchronized"

---

**Description:** This section indicates how classes are delivered—whether online, in person, or hybrid.

## 17. In-Person Requirements (Yes or No)

**Description:** This field captures if the course needs any student to be present in the class room physically or in-person.

**Description:** This field captures the enrollment option for the students as Part time or Full Time.

**Research with keywords:** "Full Time", "Part Time"

## 19. Program Managed By:

**Description:** This section identifies the department, organization, external body or the school itself is responsible for administering the certification program.

**Research with keywords:** "Center for Cybersecurity", "Cybersecurity program", "Academic programs – Cybersecurity", "Cybersecurity degree programs", "Cybersecurity advisory board"

---

## 20. NSA CAE-CD Accredited?

**Description:** This section specifies whether that particular certificate program is recognized by the National Security Agency’s Center of Academic Excellence in Cyber Defense (NSA CAECD), along with a brief explanation.

**Research with keywords:** "Accreditations", "Affiliations", "Recognitions", "Program accreditation", "Cybersecurity excellence"

---

## 21. Cert Prep (eg. Sec+, CISSP)

**Description:** This section states whether the certificate program includes preparation for industry-recognized certifications such as Security+ or CISSP. No assumptions should be made from the courses that it offers. We will only take the exact data present. For example, if the course has LINUX in it, we should not assume that it prepares for LPIC 1 certificate.

**Research with keywords:** "Security+", "CISSP", "certification preparation", "professional certifications", "industry certifications"

---

## 22. Stackable? Can Certificate Credits Transfer to a Degree?

**Description:** This section explains whether the certification can stack toward other credentials or degree programs. This field should have "Yes" or "No".

**Research with keywords:** "stackable", "stacked credential", "pathway", "modular certificate", "credit applies toward degree"

---

## 23. Stackable Details

**Description:** This section should have the details of the stackable related information provided in the previous column as "Yes" or "No". We need to put the courses names here.

---

## 24. # of Full time Faculty Members

**Description:** This section has the fulltime faculty members associated with the cybersecurity certificate program. Just the number.

**Research with keywords:** "Faculty", "Full Time Faculty"

---

## 25. Faculty Members Detailed description

**Description:** This section has the details of the Faculty. It contains the Faculty names, departments, expertise, contact details and more.

---

Required Courses:

CSCI 580: Fundamentals of Computer Networks  
CSCI 587: Cyber Physical Systems Security

## 27. Certificate Graduation Requirements

**Description:** This section will have the passing or completing of the certificate course criteria after which the certificate will be awarded to the student.

For example: Grade of C / 50% of all course work.

---

## 28. Program Curriculum

**Description:** Curriculum does not need to be a PDF. A direct webpage listing courses is sufficient. If only course names are listed (no "curriculum" heading), they can still be used. All tools may be used internally to extract course details from webpages when available. We will only give the link nothing else.

Example: https://ccsp.mines.edu/education/

---

## 29. Scholarships Available

**Description:** If scholarships details are available for that particular program offered by the education institution or any third-party institution can be considered here.

Example: https://ece.illinois.edu/academics/cybersecurity-online/master/scholarships

---

## 30. Finance Options

**Description:** Financial aid should be marked Yes only when: The specific program page clearly mentions financial aid eligibility, or The university explicitly states all courses (degree & non degree) are eligible. University wide financial aid pages without program linkage should not be assumed applicable.
